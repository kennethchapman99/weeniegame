# Boss Design Framework

> **Status: DEFERRED DESIGN REFERENCE.** Do not build boss or mission scope until Operation Pee
> Break passes its second couch-playtest gate.

Bosses should feel funny, memorable, and readable.

## Boss Rules

Every boss must:
- create role separation;
- require teamwork;
- introduce a new twist;
- escalate across phases;
- end quickly once solved.

## Boss Structure

Phase 1
Teach mechanic.

Phase 2
Mix mechanic with pressure.

Phase 3
Require teamwork.

Final Phase
Funny chaos and payoff.

## Example Bosses

### Squirrel Kingpin

Mechanics:
- fake-outs
- route switching
- stash protection

### Fence Coyote

Mechanics:
- multi-gap pressure
- lure events
- isolation attacks

### Vacuum Supreme

Mechanics:
- threat sweep
- stealth
- rescue objectives

### Nail Grinder

Mechanics:
- rhythm timing
- panic meter
- distractions

## Failure Rules

Failure should be funny.

Examples:
- squirrel taunt branch;
- dramatic couch exile;
- nail grinder victory dance;
- fake snack embarrassment.

## Boss Length

Target:
- 3 to 8 minutes.

Avoid long health-bar encounters.

Bosses are puzzle-chaos experiences, not combat encounters.
