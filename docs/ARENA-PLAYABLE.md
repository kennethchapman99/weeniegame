# Challenge & Replayability Vertical Slice — Cheddar & Cocoa

The Unity first playable has been upgraded from a simple treat arena into two prototype co-op levels generated by `ArenaBootstrap`:

- `Assets/Scenes/ArenaScene.unity` — **Level 1: Breakfast Heist**.
- `Assets/Scenes/LivingRoomChaosScene.unity` — **Level 2: Living Room Chaos**.

Both keep the verified two-dog controller/keyboard baseline and use the existing Cheddar/Cocoa, bark-ring, score, timer, treat, level-clear, and restart patterns.

## Controls

| Player | Dog | Controller | Keyboard | Bark / action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **P1** | Cheddar (golden) | pad 0, left stick | **W A S D** | controller **X** / **Space** |
| **P2** | Cocoa (brown) | pad 1, left stick | **Arrow keys** | controller **X** / **Enter** or **Right-Shift** |

Cheddar is slightly faster and has the stronger pressure-control bark. Cocoa is slower/steadier and still required for synchronized team actions.

## Level objectives and co-op mechanics

### Level 1 — Breakfast Heist / Pancake Robot pressure

- Collect breakfast plates while the **Pancake Robot Pressure** block advances.
- The pressure meter rises over time. If it overflows, the team loses score and objective progress instead of instantly failing.
- A nearby bark interrupts the robot and lowers pressure; synchronized close barks create a bigger recovery/combo.
- Winning requires objective progress reaching 100% while pressure is controlled, then the game celebrates with **BREAKFAST SAVED!**.

### Level 2 — Living Room Chaos / toy-tug coordination

- Move the shared rope toy toward the couch sunbeam by barking/tugging together within the short synchronized-bark window.
- The level uses the couch/sunbeam/toy-tug fantasy rather than another collector layout.
- Squirrel/treat chaos events periodically raise pressure and tempt players away; barking near the squirrel scares it off for recovery points.
- Winning requires repeated team actions, not just walking onto pickups.

## Scoring rules

- Treat/breakfast pickup: **+1**.
- Squirrel recovery: **+2**.
- Synchronized team bark/tug: **+3** plus combo tracking.
- Completion bonus: **25 + time remaining + 4 per team combo - 3 per mistake**.
- Pressure overflow: **-3 score** and reduced objective progress, but the round continues.

## Random round modifiers

A modifier is chosen at restart and shown in the HUD.

- **Zoomies Surge** — periodic speed bursts create slippery/reaction moments.
- **Squirrel Trouble** — squirrel distractions appear and must be barked away.
- **Pancake Panic** — pressure rises faster, especially dangerous in Breakfast Heist.

## HUD / feedback to verify

The HUD shows level name, objective, timer, score, pressure, objective progress, active modifier, team combos, recoveries, level clear, game over, and best score. Bark rings still display at each dog.

## Manual test checklist

1. Open `Assets/Scenes/ArenaScene.unity`; press Play.
2. Confirm Cheddar and Cocoa move independently with keyboard or controller.
3. Let pressure rise; confirm it visibly changes and the robot advances.
4. Bark near the robot and confirm pressure drops/recovery count changes.
5. Collect breakfast and coordinate barks until **BREAKFAST SAVED!** appears.
6. Restart and confirm score/timer/progress reset and a modifier is displayed.
7. Open `Assets/Scenes/LivingRoomChaosScene.unity`; press Play.
8. Huddle both dogs and bark within the short timing window to tug the rope toward the couch sunbeam.
9. Ignore then scare a squirrel to verify fail-soft pressure and recovery.

## Automated proof

Run all PlayMode tests:

```sh
./unity/run-playmode-tests.sh
```

The tests cover level loading, two dog spawning, independent controller input, gameplay-relevant bark state changes, pressure over time, objective completion, score rewards, restart reset, and visible random modifiers.

## Known limitations

- Objects are prototype rectangles/sprites; no paid or external assets were added.
- The shared rope uses synchronized bark as the current timing/tug input rather than a fully physical rope.
- Squirrel and Pancake Robot movement are readable pressure markers, not full character AI yet.
- Level-to-level progression UI is still manual scene loading/restart, though both upgraded scenes are in build settings.
